## Recommended Study Pattern

- 1–2 hours of focused study daily is more effective than long weekend blocks.
- Run a weekly assessment checkpoint to surface weak areas early.
- Target a 75% practice score before scheduling the real exam.
- Complete at least 2–3 full practice exams; candidates with 3+ practice exams
  pass at a markedly higher rate.

## Exam Readiness Criteria

A learner is considered exam-ready when all of the following hold:
- Overall practice score is at or above the certification pass threshold (75%).
- No individual skill area is below 65%.
- At least two timed practice exams have been completed.
- Study hours meet or exceed the certification's recommended hours.

## Common Failure Patterns

- Studying fewer than 20 hours for an intermediate certification.
- Skipping hands-on labs in favour of reading only.
- Leaving one weak skill area unaddressed until the final week.
- Scheduling the exam during a high-meeting-load week.
