# Workload and Learning Correlation (Synthetic)

> Synthetic workload analysis for demonstration only.

## Insights

- Employees with more than 20 meeting hours per week show lower study completion
  and need roughly 25% more calendar time to finish a study plan.
- Optimal completion appears when learners have 12–18 meeting hours per week and
  at least 15 focus hours available.
- Fragmented calendars (many short gaps) correlate with lower retention than a
  few protected focus blocks of 60+ minutes.

## Recommendation

- Schedule learning blocks during focus-heavy periods, not between back-to-back
  meetings.
- For learners above 20 meeting hours/week, managers should protect ~4 hours per
  week of study time and consider extending exam timelines.
- Align reminders to each learner's preferred slot rather than a fixed team-wide
  reminder time.
